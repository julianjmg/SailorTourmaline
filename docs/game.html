Terracotta

About

Terracotta is a first person POV game created by Olivia Haines that is described as “a short walk to gather your thoughts.” She created this game to help express a depression that took place over a couple of months. Her feelings and thoughts were directly reflected onto the main character of the game as the text explicitly states her thoughts.

Immersion

I found this game to be more of an experience or even a walk-through. There is no real objective or goal other than to walk and explore a beautifully created terracotta town. Terracotta is very immersive. I found the art style to be simplistic yet effective and I very much did enjoy the warm and saturated color pallet. Another note that I would like to add to the color pallet is that at the very start of the game, you are in a cool-toned purple room with only one window in it. When you leave your house and begin to explore the town, you’ll see that all the colors are very warm in contrast to the lonely dark room. This connects directly to the MC’s (main character) depression. Not only was it immersive visually, but auditorily too. Once again, in the lonely room, there are is absolutely no sound, but as soon as you leave the room, you can hear a comfortable ambient noise which consists of a light bird chirping and wind sounds. Once you pass through the town and make it to the Cat Café, a Lofi type of beat begins to play which is very relaxing. Not only are the graphics and sounds of the game immersive, but they also play into the pattern that the MC is accepting their feelings more as the game progresses.

Character

As we progress through the game, we get to learn more and more about the MC. We find out that they have been struggling with depression and burnout from work as well. I like that the MC doesn’t have a name or character model because it allows more people to be able to relate to the character and truly go through the game as themselves.

Mechanics

I did not find Terracotta to be challenging at all, but I was not expecting it to be. This game was made for the developer to self-express. It was not made to appeal to gamers (the average demographic,) which Is what I often see. But back to the challenge of the game. The way to “beat” the game is to explore the whole town and make it to the Cat Café. Along the way, there are barriers that will keep you from progressing until you have explored each area. When you walk in close proximity to an area that you have not explored, text will appear and share the MC’s thoughts. While I really do enjoy this game and think it was beautifully well done, I thought of a game mechanic that could work for Terracotta. The idea is that there would be items that you could collect like a key that would get you through each area. Currently, there are barriers that are drawn as diagonal lines and if keys were implemented, then maybe physical doors or even gates could be added.

Kumu Network

I created a kumu graph to see what kind of relationship there was between the objects/drawings and the different locations of the game. The same pattern where the MC accepts their feelings more as the game progresses is also illustrated on this graph. I made my kumu network to look similar to a game roadmap so that you can see which objects/drawings are present in each area. Based on this network, I was able to find that are more animated drawings as you progress through the game. As I had said previously, there was a tone shift from cool colors to warm colors and also from no sound to ambient sounds to a Lofi song. In this example however, I think it’s a bit more literal as the word animated can mean lively. In the very first room, there were no animated drawings, but at the Cat Café, every single animated drawing was there. Thus, meaning that from this walk and formation of thoughts, the MC was able to accept their feelings and even feeling better.

Final Thoughts

Overall, I really enjoyed this game and I think that it's so well done and simply beautiful. I love that it was made for self-expression and I feel that many people who play it will feel validated for having similar emotions as the MC.
